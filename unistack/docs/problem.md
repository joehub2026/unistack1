# Problem Statement — UniStack

## Context
INES-Ruhengeri is a university in Musanze, Rwanda with hundreds of students who actively
trade goods, seek housing, and share information. The absence of a structured digital platform
forces all this activity onto WhatsApp, a messaging app not designed for marketplace use.

## Core Problem
**Students have no safe, organized, and searchable platform to buy/sell items, find housing,
or share notices within the INES community.**

## Root Causes
| Cause | Impact |
|---|---|
| No central platform | Information scattered across multiple WhatsApp groups |
| No identity verification | Scammers can pose as students |
| No moderation | False listings, spam, and inappropriate content spread freely |
| No archiving | Old posts vanish; no searchable history |
| No categorization | Users must scroll through unrelated messages |

## Consequence
- Students lose money to scams
- Genuine sellers lose buyers due to poor visibility
- Important announcements get buried
- No accountability for bad actors

## Proposed Solution
A web-based, role-protected, moderated digital notice board and marketplace exclusively
for the INES community — verified by school email, moderated by appointed staff, and
administrated by IT/management.
